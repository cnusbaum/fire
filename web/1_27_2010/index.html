While taxpayers have long valued Arizona's first responders, the same cannot be said of some Arizona politicians. Having spent the state deep into the red, now they're taking dead aim at pensions for fire fighters and police officers. If these politicians have their way, the Arizona Public Safety Retirement System will undergo drastic changes, leaving Arizona's first responders far worse off than they are today.

Meanwhile, being a fire fighter is more dangerous than ever. Each year in America, between 80 and 115 firefighters are killed on the job, a statistic that doesn't include many more deaths attributable to hazardous chemical exposure and infectious diseases. Another 32,000 to 40,000 fire fighters are injured on scene, responding to 911 calls.










  


Let's not gut the middle-class retirement our Arizona fire fighters were promised upon being hired. Get armed with the facts and get active today.
