GREEDY? HARDLY.

DON'T LET POLITICIANS GUT PUBLIC SAFETY RETIREMENT

Arizon's fire fighters and police officers risk their lives and bodies to protect us.

The average annual salary of an Arizona public safety worker? $39,000.

Public safety retirees rely heavily on the middle-class pensions they were promised when they were hired. Unfortunately, some politicians in the Arizona Legislature want to gut these benefits. They've accused fire fighters and police officers of being greedy. The politicians say fire and police are getting rich by robbing taxpayers. That they're pigs at the trough.

Tell that to fire retirees from Gila River, where pensions average $23,959 yearly. Or fire retirees from Pinetop, where pensions average $25,263 per year.

Imagine living off that sum while paying for health insurance that can cost a family upwards of $20,000 each year?

If the legislators get their way, public safety retirees will lose the cost of living allowances they depend on to keep their families above water. And the average Arizona fire fighter or cop - who's likely already been hit with pay cuts and furlough days - will see another yearly pay cut of about $1,600.
